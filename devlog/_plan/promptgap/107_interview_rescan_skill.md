# 107 — Interview / Rescan Skill Prompt Gaps

Gap class: PROMPT/SKILL · evidence: explorer #107

> codexclaw shipped the interview runtime, but the **agent-facing prose contract** is still much thinner than jaw's interview discipline. The gap is not mostly in hook plumbing; it is in what the prompt surface explicitly forces the main session to do, refuse, re-check, and present to the user.

## Reinforcement shape

1. Expand `plugins/codexclaw/skills/interview/SKILL.md` from runtime-status notes into an actual operator manual: question-quality rubric, negativity bias, readiness explanation, and closeout options.
2. Upgrade `MIND_DISPATCH_DIRECTIVE` from ownership prose to loop doctrine: `scan -> triage -> ask -> apply -> rescan -> explicit proceed/refine choice`, with a mandatory final scan before any I->P transition.
3. Add jaw-style "why this question now" and "assumption, not feature list" wording to the injected directive text so the main session cannot satisfy Interview with generic clarifying chatter.
4. Replace the loose "ask the user to proceed or keep interviewing" line with a numbered fork that keeps Interview as the owner until the user explicitly chooses `Proceed to Plan` or `More interview`.
5. Tighten freeze/approval language around codexclaw's actual model: plan/devlog may evolve during Interview, but they are still draft interview artifacts; execution and implementation remain out of bounds until Interview closes and Plan takes over.
